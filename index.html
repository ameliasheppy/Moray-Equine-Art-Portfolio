What does a horse mean to you?

Have you ever looked, really looked at a horse and just let the absolute beauty wash over you? Have you ever watched a foal attempting to gallop by it's mom and fallen in love with their little baby antics? Have you been the proud rider at a show, or the parent anxiously watching their child take lessons, and held your breath as the bond between animal and human was on display for a crowd to see and you to feel? This is the place for you! At Moray Equine Art, I am fascinated by all things a horse does, and I try to capture that in a model horse. Just one moment in time, memorialized on an object you can have for a lifetime.

How do we do this?

Choose a photo reference, or actual horse to base the model on. Next step is take the model down to as basic of a canvas as we desire. In some cases, we completely chop the horse up into pieces and rebuild. For the model below, I knew she needed a new tail, ears, mane, and most of a face. More details on how I proceeded from this point are on the My Work page. But this is how most artists begin. This allows us to create the model how you want it, in minute detail.
